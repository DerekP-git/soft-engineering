History of Morningside Tennis

Founded: 1924.

Home Court: Mason Family Tennis Complex

Team Colors: Maroon and Black

Mascot: Mustang

1901: Tennis gets added to the Athletics Association - The Tennis Association is formed and is headed by President and Manager, Ross P. Brown.

1906: Ladies Tennis club Formed -

1909: Tennis Courts Open - The residents of Renaissance hall are rejoicing over the fact that, in a few days, a tennis court will adorn their grounds. The court is to be situated in the northwest corner of the yard and will be surrounded on three sides by the beautiful pine hedge which makes these grounds the most artistic site for a tennis court.

1921: New Tennis Court - The third court is to the west of the other courts and has been ready for play for several years as far as the fence being up was concerned, but until this fall the weeds have never been cleaned off of the plot and the rectangle rolled or put into shape.

1924: Women's Athletics Association is founded - Tennis gets added to the Women's Athletics Association in order to promote physical fitness among women.

1960: No candidates for competition - This year the Maroon Chiefs did not field a tennis team for the first time in several years. With only two lettermen returning and no other candidates available it was decided to forego varsity competition. However, Gene Quilleash and Bobby Walker entered the conference tournament for the Chiefs. A promising group of freshmen should enable the resumption of tennis in 1961

1974: First year for Woman's Tennis - The first year of women's tennis at Morningside was short. The season ended with a 1-2 record. The women fought to two losses at Briar Cliff, but won their other game at Buena Vista.

1982: Women's Tennis Places 5th In Tourney - The Morningside women's tennis team completed their season with a 5th place in the Io-Kota Conference Tournament. Jill Tasker and Becky Vermilyea were both undefeated in singles action during the regular season.

1984: Women's tennis team places second in IO-KOTA - The women's tennis team improved with the depth of new players to a 3-3 record for second place at the IO-KOTA Conference Tournament held at Westmar in October.

2008: Men's tennis team tied for second in GPAC -
